The Exercise
You were born with a unique life purpose. This exercise reveals it by looking at how you see other people. Name three people you deeply admire and the one quality you admire most in each of them. Then name three people you strongly dislike and the one quality you cannot stand about each of them. Be honest. Don't filter. The exercise only works if you tell the truth.

Your Golden Shadow
The qualities you admire in others are qualities that already exist inside you. You could not recognize them in someone else if they were not already part of who you are. These are the parts of your potential that you may have buried, whether because they were not encouraged, not safe, or not recognized as you grew up.
Part of your life's purpose is to stop projecting these qualities onto others and begin to own them yourself. When you do, your motivation rises, distractions fade, and life becomes deeply fulfilling because your soul is finally being expressed.
🌑
Your Dark Mirror
The qualities you judge in others are qualities you have disowned in yourself. This is not about becoming like the people you dislike. It is about acknowledging that these qualities have a healthy expression, and your work is to find it.
What we resist, persists. It takes enormous energy to keep these qualities repressed. When you bring them into the light and find their healthy form, that energy becomes available to you again. Your struggles lose their grip because you are no longer fighting parts of yourself.
Reframe each quality below. What is the healthy, mature version of the quality you dislike? Every shadow has a gift hidden inside it.
🔥
Claim It
These qualities are not aspirations. They are not goals you are working toward. They are already inside you, waiting to be acknowledged. The moment you stop attributing them to other people and start owning them as your own, everything changes.
