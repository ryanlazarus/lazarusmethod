Overhead Deep Squat Challenge
Patient: —Date: —
A simple yet powerful way to assess your body’s mobility, stability, and movement control. This single test reveals tightness, asymmetries, and compensation patterns that can limit performance or increase injury risk.
1
Setup

Stand barefoot with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointing forward.

Hold a broomstick, dowel, or straight object overhead with a wide grip.

Keep your elbows locked and your arms in line with your ears.

Record yourself from the side or use a mirror.

2
Perform the Movement

Engage your core gently — think “brace but breathe.”

Slowly lower into a deep squat while keeping your arms overhead.

Go as low as you can without your heels lifting or your arms falling forward.

Pause for 1–2 seconds at the bottom, then rise back to standing.

Repeat 3 times.

3
Grade Yourself (0–3 Scale)
ScoreMovement QualityWhat It Means
3
Optimal		Heels flat, torso upright, arms stay overhead, thighs below parallel.Excellent mobility & control.
2
Functional but Limited		Heels stay down, but torso leans forward or arms drift slightly.Mild restrictions in hips, ankles, or shoulders.
1
Dysfunctional		Heels lift, knees cave in/out, or arms drop forward.Significant mobility or stability issues.
0
Pain		Any discomfort or pain during the movement.Stop & seek assessment.
Quick Clues to Identify Your Limitation
Heels lift: Tight calves or restricted ankle dorsiflexion.
Arms fall forward: Tight lats or limited thoracic mobility.
Torso leans forward: Weak core or tight hips.
Knees cave in: Weak glutes or hip instability.
