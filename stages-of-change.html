The Anatomy of Change

Behavior change is not an event. It is a process that unfolds over time, involving progress through a series of stages. While progression can occur in a linear fashion, a nonlinear path is common. You may recycle through stages or regress to earlier ones from later ones. That is normal.

Certain principles and processes of change work best at each stage to reduce resistance, facilitate progress, and prevent relapse. These include decisional balance, self-efficacy, and the processes of change. The first step is recognizing where you currently stand.

The Six Stages
Pre-Contemplation
Not yet acknowledging that there is a problem behavior that needs to be changed.
Contemplation
Acknowledging that there is a problem, but not yet ready or willing to make a change.
Preparation
Getting ready to change. Researching, planning, and building the intention to act.
Action
Actively changing behavior. Putting in the work, day by day, to build new patterns.
Maintenance
Sustaining the behavior change over time. The new pattern is becoming part of who you are.
Relapse
Returning to older behaviors and abandoning the new changes. This is not failure. It is part of the process.
