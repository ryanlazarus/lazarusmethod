How to Read the Target
Think of this like an archery target. The green center is the bullseye where the most nutrient-dense, highest-quality foods live. The yellow ring contains foods that are good but not optimal. The red outer ring is the danger zone where heavily processed, inflammatory, and nutrient-poor foods sit. The closer you eat to the center, the better you fuel your body. The Nourish Target covers all three macronutrients: Proteins (25-35% of daily calories), Fats (45-65%), and Carbohydrates (10-20%).
🔴
Your Challenge
Remove all red-zone foods for at least one full day. Just one day. Look at the outer ring of each macronutrient section below and commit to avoiding those foods for 24 hours. Track your red-free days below. Once one day feels easy, try two in a row. Then three. Build momentum.

What Happens When You Remove the Red Zone
Each day you stay out of the red zone, something changes inside your body. Check off your days above and watch what unlocks below.
Day
1
Blood Sugar Stabilizes
Without processed sugars, refined flours, and seed oils spiking your glucose, your pancreas gets a break from overproducing insulin. Fewer energy crashes. Less of that mid-afternoon wall. Your body starts running on steady fuel instead of a sugar rollercoaster.
Day
2
Brain Fog Begins to Lift
Processed foods drive neuroinflammation by allowing inflammatory cytokines to cross the blood-brain barrier and disrupt neurotransmitter function. As those foods clear your system, dopamine and serotonin signaling stabilizes. Focus sharpens, mental clarity improves, and that foggy, scattered feeling starts to fade.
Day
3
Inflammation Starts Cooling Down
Red-zone foods are some of the most inflammatory substances you can put in your body. Within days of removing them, your body begins downregulating its inflammatory response. Joint stiffness, puffiness, and that general feeling of being swollen start to ease.
🔒
3 more benefits unlock at Day 5. Keep going.
Day
5
Your Gut Microbiome Begins to Shift
The sugar-loving bacteria in your gut (including Candida yeast) start losing their food supply and dying off. Beneficial species like Bifidobacterium and Lactobacillus begin to gain ground. Bloating, gas, and digestive discomfort often decrease noticeably. Your gut is recalibrating.
Day
6
Cravings Weaken, Energy Steadies
Your body is becoming metabolically flexible, relearning how to burn stored fat for fuel instead of relying on constant sugar hits. Cravings that felt unbearable on Day 1 lose their grip. Sleep improves as blood sugar stops spiking and crashing at night. You wake up feeling genuinely rested.
Day
7
Systemic Benefits Compound
Skin starts clearing as inflammatory load drops. Insulin sensitivity measurably improves. Mood stabilizes as the gut-brain axis communication strengthens. Your taste buds recalibrate so real food starts tasting better. This is where people say they feel like a different person. The longer you stay out of the red, the more these benefits stack.
